# MEG Training Checklist

### **<span style="color:blue">Entering the MEG Lab</span>**

- Having undergone on-line CHBH Building Induction and after being granted secure corridor access and requested Training, MEG Operators are shown the location of the MEG key safe, informed of the combination, and of the importance of locking the Lab and returning the key/s to the safe at EoD.

### **<span style="color:blue">MEG Basics</span>**

**MEG Sensors / MSR**

- The **[Basic Workings](../hardware/meg.md)** of the MEG are explained (the type of sensor, the MSR, Helium cooling to ensure superconductivity).
- MEG Operators are told what not to bring into the **[MSR](../hardware/safety.md)** and the reasons why.
- MEG Operators are shown the workings of the MSR door (via compressed air), and how to open/close the door, in normal operation.
- MEG Operators are shown how to **[open the MSR door](../hardware/msr.md)** in an emergency. 

**Helium recycling system**

- The '''[[Helium Recycling System]]''' is discussed and MEG operators are shown where the IHR system is located.
- The various gantry positions are shown, and instructions are given on how to adjust the gantry. See '''[https://www.chbh.bham.ac.uk/wiki/index.php/Holiday_Cover_/_IHR_Key_User#Moving_the_Gantry Moving the Gantry]'''
- MEG Operators are informed about what position they must leave the gantry at EoD, and it is explained why this is important.
- All MEG Operators are shown to be able to move the gantry into the various in-use positions themselves.
- MEG Operators are informed about the Recycling Schedule/MTB, and to (ideally) keep to the slot times mentioned in '''[[MEG Bookings]]'''

### **<span style="color:blue">MSR Equipment</span>**

**Gantry Chair**

- MEG Operators are shown how to use/operate/raise the chair. 
- MEG Operators are shown where the seating supplies are (cushions/foam/blankets etc).
- MEG Operators are shown how to help a participant into the chair, and how to plug in the various leads.
- MEG Operators are informed that participants, once relaxed, may need to be raised higher (chair pumped up more) in the gantry to ensure a better HPI check.
- MEG Operators are informed to be aware and take care of/help up participants out of the chair at the end of the experiment as they may be unsteady.

**Participant Intercom & Video Monitoring System**

- MEG Operators are shown how to turn on/use the MSR intercom, and for the need to provide clear instructions to the participant.
- MEG Operators are shown how to use/modify the MSR camera display monitor on the Control Room wall.

### **<span style="color:blue">Consumables, Manuals</span>**

- MEG Operators are informed of the location of the various MEG '''[https://www.chbh.bham.ac.uk/wiki/index.php/Location_of_Consumables Consumables]''' items and User '''[[Manuals]]'''/Documentation

### **<span style="color:blue">Stimulus Equipment</span>**

- MEG Operators are shown how to log into the OLD Stimulus PC (NEW Stimulus PC automatically logs on), and how to restart either as necessary (if PROPixx DP to DVI Adapter needs rebooting).
- MEG Operators are shown where/how to turn on the PROPixx Projector (if required), and how to wake and sleep the Projector (lens cap to be left off now).
- MEG Operators are shown how to set up/use the NAtA Button boxes, and where the Converter Box is located (Stimulus Cabinet).
- MEG Operators are shown how to use the L2V Converters and where the control boxes are located.
- MEG Operators are shown how to use the EyeLink PC, how to '''[https://www.chbh.bham.ac.uk/wiki/index.php/EyeLink_Operations_Guide Position/Use]''' the FO Camera Head and how to use/charge the EyeLink batteries, both '''[https://www.chbh.bham.ac.uk/wiki/index.php/Charging_new_EyeLink_batteries New]''' and '''[https://www.chbh.bham.ac.uk/wiki/index.php/Charging_EyeLink_batteries Old]'''.
- MEG Operators are shown the two auditory stimulus systems available, and how to use both.
- MEG Operators are shown the Digitimer DS7A Stimulator, and, if required, how to use it safely.

### **<span style="color:blue">Before the Participant arrives</span>**

**MEG Operators are instructed about performing various house-keeping tasks:**

- To check the gantry position.
- To make sure their experiment performs as expected/triggers arrive at the MEG. 
- To make sure all devices work (button boxes/EyeLink).

### **<span style="color:blue">Preparing the Participant</span>**

- MEG Operators are reminded to have the participant fill in an '''[https://www.chbh.bham.ac.uk/chbh/chbh-documents-and-forms/meg-informed-consent-form-v_4_1.pdf informed consent form]''' (including checks for pacemakers, etc.).
- MEG Operators are shown how to use the Participant Logging Computer.
- MEG Operators are reminded that they and the participant have to remove all metal: e.g. phones, keys, belts, (metal filled) bras, make-up, earrings and piercings, zippers and metal buttons, preferably no braces/metal fillings/glasses. Two types of metal detectors are provided, and their usage explained
- MEG Operators are instructed to give the participant the '''[[metal checklist for the participant]]''' to check themselves if they have removed all metal.
- MEG Operators are shown that there are metal-free blue Hospital Scrubs available for the participant to change into (different sizes on labeled Changing Room wardrobe shelves), and where to place used scrubs ready for washing.
- MEG Operators are instructed to emphasize that a participant can always stop/ask questions or communicate, via the intercom, once sitting in the gantry/MSR.

**Bedside manners**

- MEG Operators are reminded to always make participants feel comfortable, to be polite & courteous at all times, to check whether the participant is feeling OK, paying attention (via the Monitoring System) for symptoms of claustrophobia (e.g. sweating, nervous behavior).<br /> 
Ask if they need to take a break, would like or a drink of water. Water, in plastic bottles, is available.

### **<span style="color:blue">Acquisition of Data</span>**

- MEG Operators are shown how to access/login to the DACQ Workstation.
- MEG operators are shown how to start up/use the Acquisition software.
	- e.g. How to create projects, enter Participant data, set up MEG channels/triggers.
- MEG Operators are informed as to why HPI coils are required, shown how to attach '''[https://www.chbh.bham.ac.uk/wiki/index.php/Using_Tegaderm_Tape HPI coils]''', and how to use the ''Polhemus'' to perform a HPI digitization.
	 - The Phantom (''Phantom Phyllis'') can be used to try out/practice digitizations on).
- MEG Operators are shown where/how to attach EOG, ECG electrodes, and the types of electrodes that are available for use.
- MEG Operators are shown the location of the EEG caps and informed how to '''[https://www.chbh.bham.ac.uk/wiki/index.php/Correct_Positioning/Usage_of_EEG_Caps use]''' them and how to check the '''[https://www.chbh.bham.ac.uk/wiki/index.php/Correct_Positioning/Usage_of_EEG_Caps#Check_impedances impedances]''' afterward.
- MEG Operators are shown how to use the Tuner software to check sensor noise levels, and how to resolve noisy channels.
- MEG Operators are advised, due to 32bit software, single saved-file size restricted to 4GB (~20min).
	 - Experimental Paradigms may need to be adjusted to avoid spanning of acquired data into multiple files, requiring stitching afterward.

### **<span style="color:blue">Copying Data</span>**

- MEG Operators are informed they need their own '''[https://www.chbh.bham.ac.uk/wiki/index.php/Copying_MEG_data RDS Project Space & Linux account]''' to copy off acquired data.
- MEG Operators are shown '''[https://www.chbh.bham.ac.uk/wiki/index.php/Copying_MEG_data how to copy off acquired data to RDS]'''

### **<span style="color:blue">Hygiene, Tidying up</span>**

- MEG Operators are told they need to clean their hands with alcohol gel before, and after, touching the participant ('''[https://www.chbh.bham.ac.uk/wiki/index.php/Using_Tegaderm_Tape attaching HPI coils]''', EOG electrodes etc.)
- MEG Operators are shown how to '''[https://www.chbh.bham.ac.uk/wiki/index.php/Correct_Positioning/Usage_of_EEG_Caps use]''' and '''[https://www.chbh.bham.ac.uk/wiki/index.php/Cleaning_EEG_caps,_Make-up/EEG_Gel_removal clean]''' EEG caps, reusable electrodes.
- MEG Operators are informed how and why to '''[https://www.chbh.bham.ac.uk/wiki/index.php/Tidying_Up tidy up]''' (e.g. all equipment is shared).
- MEG Operators are told of importance to clean communally used areas with alcohol gel, alcohol wipes (e.g. gantry helmet, chair arms & tray, NAtA buttons (using wipes NOT gel!).

### **<span style="color:blue">Log book, Lab Safety, and reporting of incidents/problems</span>**

- MEG Operators are instructed to fill in the '''[https://www.chbh.bham.ac.uk/chbh/chbh-documents-and-forms/meg_measurement_log_information_v1_2c.pdf Log book]''' after any MEG Lab usage, however small. This is now mandatory as part of the MEG '''[https://www.chbh.bham.ac.uk/chbh/chbh-documents-and-forms/oplr/part-d-meg-fault-reporting-procedures OPLR]''' Fault Reporting Procedure.
- MEG Operators are informed of the location of the two Oxygen Depletion Alarms, and what to do if they sound.
- MEG Operators are shown the location of support telephone contact numbers, the Lab first aid box, the location of the nearest Fire Alarm Call Point and Fire Exit, and, as necessary, to follow the Emergency Procedures as laid out in the MEG '''[https://www.chbh.bham.ac.uk/chbh/chbh-documents-and-forms/oplr/part-d-meg-emergency-procedures OPLR].'''
- MEG Operators are informed of the MEG Emergency Evacuation Plan ('''[https://www.chbh.bham.ac.uk/wiki/buic-files/MEG/MEG_Emergency_Evacuation_Plan.pdf MEEP]''') and the procedures are gone through.
- MEG Operators are instructed to contact the MEG Support Officer by 'phone or email, of any MEG-related issues or problems.
